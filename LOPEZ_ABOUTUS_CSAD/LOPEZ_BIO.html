Ang Napakahaba Kong Biography


Childhood Years

My name is Virgilio Lopez Jr., but my nickname is Jev.1 I was born in Batangas City during a chilly night on November 20, 2000.2 My grandmother was with my parents at the time.3 She was also there when my mother was giving birth to me, and when she saw me, she exclaimed that I looked exactly like my dad.4 That was how I got my full name.5 Growing up, I was a very cheerful kid.6 I was also very small, and I had big, drooping cheeks.7 I went to a small private school during pre-elementary and elementary.8 Although the school was small, it had an open space which was very convenient for us.9 After classes were over, me and my classmates would always play a lot.10 We would run around playing games like patintero, habulan, plisiner, and many others.11 After that, I would go home and watch TV.12 During the Christmas vacation, me and my family would go to Ilocos Sur, which was the home province of my parents.13 We would spend our vacation there with all of my relatives which also came from other places.14 Even though we do it every year, I still have so much fun during my stay there.15 Aside from our stay there, the long travel is what I look forward to.16 It takes about 8 hours to travel by car from Batangas City to Ilocos Sur in the past.17 Although it may seem boring, it is actually the opposite.18 As a kid, I was very fascinated with what was going on around me.19 There are also a lot of interesting places we see along the way.20 And although it may be tiring, it was very worth it every year.21

After classes were over, we would run around playing games like patintero, habulan, plisiner, and many others.

Teenage Years

In high school, I went to Batangas State University (BSU).1 During my early years there, I wasn't very much focused in studying.2 All I wanted to do was to go to the nearby computer shop and play video games.3 I would even sneak out of the school during programs or events, which there was a lot of in our school.4 Slowly, my grades suffered.5 I even got a failing grade in one of the easiest subjects because I wasn't submitting requirements.6 My parents often scolded me because of this, and when they found out I was spending most of my time playing video games.7 One time, I even got punished with washing the dishes for a month.8 At first, not much changed and I was still very lazy, although I did stop going to computer shops altogether.9 However, I grew tired of constantly being scolded.10 I decided to change my habits and persevered in my studies.11 I also made sure to work on my requirements really hard and pass it on time.12 All those efforts paid off, and I was constantly ranking first in my section.13 Before I knew it, I was finally graduating high school.14 In senior high school, my parents suggested that I study in Metro Manila because BSU doesn't have Senior High.15 So I took the entrance exam in Makati Science High School.16 Luckily, I got accepted, and thus began the hardest 2 years of my life.17 At first, I was culture shocked because it was very different to what I was used to.18 I was barely able to keep up with the lessons.19 Fortunately, I wasn't alone in struggling.20 Ultimately, however, I was able to adapt with my new environment, and I also graduated with flying colors.21 Looking back now, I had so much fun during those two years.22

I decided to change my habits and persevered in my studies.

College Years

After graduating from Senior High School, I was having second thoughts whether to continue my studies in Manila or return to Batangas.1 At first, I made up my mind to return to Batangas.2 So I didn't take any entrance exams in Metro Manila.3 However, during the summer vacation, I realized that it was better to study in Manila.4 So I scrambled to look for universities that still had open entrance exams.5 Luckily, there were still several universities offering exams, so I took them all.6 Ultimately, I enrolled in University of the East (UE) Manila.7 I chose UE because I was able to get a scholarship there.8 I would have wanted badly to go to DLSU, but the tuition fee is very expensive.9 We wouldn't be able to afford it unless we borrow money from some relatives.10 Initially, I didn't like it in UE because the campus wasn't that huge.11 I also hated the commute to Manila because it was very crowded and polluted.12 It was noisy and too congested.13 On the other hand, it is very convenient because I only need to take one jeepney ride from home to school.14 To make it even more fun, I decided to buy a bicycle and ride it instead of commuting.15 I've been using it everyday before the pandemic hit, and it was very refreshing.16 It was a good start to my day and it gave me energy for the whole day.17 However, sometimes I tend to wake up very late.18 In those cases, I just commute again to and from school.19 I just hope that this pandemic would be over soon.20 I really miss going to school, biking, and a lot of other things.21
