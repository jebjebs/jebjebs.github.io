How to prevent hair from damage

By Patricia Lantin | trisha.lantin@gmail.com

STEP 1

Wait a few days between washing your hair.

If you're in the habit of washing your hair every day, give your hair a rest since it strips natural oils from your hair. Waiting an extra day or two keeps your hair moisturized and protected.

  • Struggling to skip the shampoo session? Apply a dry shampoo product to your roots if you feel like your hair is greasy or oily.

  • Aim to wash your hair 2 to 3 times a week.

STEP 2

Use detangling products after washing and conditioning to protect your hair from damage.

Frizzy hair tangles together much easier than smooth, healthy hair. To make it easier to brush or comb your hair, spray or brush a detangling product from the ends of your hair to the roots after you finish conditioning your hair. If you don't have a leave-in detangling product, you'll need to rinse out the detangler with warm water.

  • Plan on using a detangling product every time you wash your hair. Consider it an extra line of defense from future damage!

STEP 3

Air dry your hair to prevent it from becoming frizzy.

Permed hair is already prone to dry, frizzy ends, but you'll only make it worse if you blow-dry your hair after washing it. Instead, blot your hair with a T-shirt and let it dry naturally.

  • You'll protect your permed hair from even more heat damage.

STEP 4

Avoid styling your hair with heat.

If you've changed your mind about a curly or wavy perm, you might be tempted to straighten it with a flat iron or curling iron, but don't! Your hair has already been through a big treatment and more heat will damage it further. Wait until your hair is healthy, soft, and elastic before you style with heat.

  • If you have to use a blow dryer or heat source to do your hair, put it on the coolest temperature setting.

STEP 4

Skip coloring treatments until your hair is healthy and nourished.

If you're not happy with your hair or you just want a change, you may want to color it. Unfortunately, chemical color dyes are harsh and if your hair is still damaged from the perm, it can make frizziness, thinning, and breakages even worse.

  • Not sure if your hair is healthy enough to dye? Ask your hairstylist for their opinion or play it safe and wait until your hair feels as soft and strong as it used to.

STEP 5

Protect your hair from sunlight.

UV rays from sunlight can really harm your hair, so avoid sunlight or wear a hat or scarf outside. For an extra layer of protection, spritz your hair with a hair protecting spray that includes sodium laneth, hydrolyzed protein, and silicone before you go out.

  • Follow up with extra conditioning if you're outside for a while. Between the heat and the sun, your hair will probably be even dryer than usual.

STEP 6

Trim your hair to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.

Is your perm wispy and unruly at the ends? Unfortunately, a bad perm can damage your hair so you have ends that stick out straight below the curls or waves. Look closely at your ends and snip away any that are split or stick out noticeably. You don't have to trim a lot—about 1⁄2 inch (1.3 cm) will remove the split ends and encourage your hair to grow.

  • Although you can do this yourself, it might be easier to ask a friend to help you trim the back of your hair.
